# Legal Research Notes

Status: working reference notes for product compliance, not final legal advice.
Last updated: 2026-06-18.

These notes capture the main external reference points used for the first legal/compliance draft package.

## Investment Advice / Licensed Professional Risk

Reference:

- SEC Investor.gov, "Investor Bulletin: How to Select an Investment Professional": https://www.investor.gov/introduction-investing/general-resources/news-alerts/alerts-bulletins/investor-bulletins/investor-0

Product takeaway:

- Be careful with language that makes Market Signal AI look like a licensed investment professional.
- Avoid implying that the product selects investments for a user, gives personal recommendations, manages investments, or acts in a fiduciary/advisory role.
- Keep outputs framed as informational research and require users to make independent decisions.

## Subscription / Automatic Renewal Risk

Reference:

- FTC, "Click to Cancel: The FTC's amended Negative Option Rule and what it means for your business": https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/blog/2024/10/click-cancel-ftcs-amended-negative-option-rule-what-it-means-your-business

Product takeaway:

- Subscription terms must be clear before checkout.
- Automatic renewal requires clear disclosure and informed user consent.
- Cancellation must be easy to find and complete.
- Trial-to-paid conversion needs clear timing, price, and cancellation disclosure.

## Telegram Data Risk

Reference:

- Telegram Privacy Policy: https://telegram.org/privacy

Product takeaway:

- Telegram is a separate platform and may process user data under its own terms.
- Bot developers may receive user data when users interact with bots, press buttons, use inline bots, participate in groups with bots, or use bot payment features.
- Market Signal AI should tell users not to send sensitive credentials, payment card data, trading account passwords, or private keys through Telegram.

## Payment Provider / Refund Flow

Reference:

- Stripe Consumer Terms: https://stripe.com/legal/consumer

Product takeaway:

- Payment-provider terms do not replace Market Signal AI's own refund policy.
- The product should clearly tell users how refunds are requested, how disputes are handled, and which charges are generally non-refundable unless law requires otherwise.
