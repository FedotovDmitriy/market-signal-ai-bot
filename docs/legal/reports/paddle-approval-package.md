## 5. Refund And Cancellation Package

### Cancellation policy

Customer-facing position:

- Users can cancel through the billing/account interface or Paddle Customer Portal.
- Cancellation stops future renewals.
- Paid access may continue until the end of the current paid billing period unless the plan or provider flow states otherwise.
- After the billing period ends, paid features such as private ticker analysis, Telegram delivery and API access may be disabled or downgraded.

Ready-to-use wording:

> You can cancel your subscription through the billing portal. Canceling stops future renewals. Your current paid access may remain active until the end of the current billing period unless stated otherwise at checkout.

### Refund policy

Customer-facing position:

- Subscription fees are generally non-refundable after the billing period begins, unless required by law or approved case by case.
- Duplicate charges, confirmed billing errors, unauthorized charges and material technical failures may be reviewed.
- Annual plan refunds may be reviewed case by case, especially during MVP.
- Refunds must follow Paddle's process as Merchant of Record.
- Chargebacks may lead to temporary suspension while the dispute is investigated.

Ready-to-use wording:

> Refund requests are reviewed under our Refund Policy, Paddle's Merchant of Record checkout rules, and applicable law. Duplicate charges, confirmed billing errors, unauthorized charges and material service failures may be reviewed case by case.

### Support route

Required before approval:

- billing support email;
- refund support workflow;

- support response owner;
- target response time for billing issues.

## 8. Payment Risk Review

### Risk: regulated financial advice perception

Risk:

Paid ticker analytics may be misunderstood as financial advice or trading recommendations.

Controls:

- no personalized advice;
- no portfolio-based recommendations;
- no buy/sell/hold instructions;
- no guaranteed returns;
- no broker/execution integration;
- visible risk disclaimer at pricing, checkout, dashboard, first analysis and reports;
- Terms prohibit advisory, resale, redistribution, broker execution, auto-trading and copy-trading use without a separate agreement.

Residual status:

- Acceptable for Paddle review as informational SaaS, subject to provider approval and securities perimeter review.

### Risk: chargebacks and refund disputes

Risk:

Subscription products can generate disputes if renewal, cancellation or refund terms are unclear.

Controls:

- clear recurring billing wording before checkout;
- Paddle Customer Portal for cancellation and billing management;
- cancellation at least as easy as signup;
- clear refund policy;
- billing support route;
- audit record of terms/risk acceptance;
- subscription status shown in account.

Residual status:

- Manageable if Paddle portal and clear checkout wording are used.

### Risk: tax/VAT/sales tax complexity

Risk:

Global SaaS subscriptions may trigger VAT, GST, sales tax and invoice obligations.

Controls:

- use Paddle as Merchant of Record for first paid launch;
- avoid direct Stripe in MVP;
- no direct off-platform sales until separate tax review;
- CPA reconciles Paddle reports monthly.

Residual status:

- Reduced by MoR model; company income tax, bookkeeping, local tax residency and corporate filings remain.

### Risk: failed payments and access mismatch

Risk:

Users may retain access after failed payments, cancellation, refund or chargeback if lifecycle mapping is incomplete.

Controls:

- use Paddle webhooks for subscription lifecycle;
- Core maps subscription states to access;
- store provider event IDs and provider environment;
- QA lifecycle states before live launch;
- disable or downgrade paid access after expired/canceled/refunded/chargeback state where appropriate.

Residual status:

- Blocked until provider event ledger, webhook config and QA are complete.

### Risk: API misuse by B2B customers

Risk:

API customers may display outputs as regulated advice, resell results or create trading tools.

Controls:

- API Terms required before first API key;
- commercial redistribution, white-label, paid signals, advisory use, broker/execution use and resale require separate written agreement;
- outputs must keep attribution and disclaimers;
- API quotas and usage monitoring.

Residual status:

- Manageable for MVP if API use remains limited and gated by Terms.

## 11. Application Answer Bank

### What does your product do?

Market Signal AI is a SaaS platform for informational market analytics. It provides ticker analysis, market summaries, news-to-ticker matching, risk notes, analytical reports, Telegram delivery and optional API access depending on subscription plan.

### Is it financial advice?

No. Market Signal AI does not provide personalized investment advice, financial advice, brokerage, portfolio management, trade execution, auto-trading or copy trading. All outputs are general informational market analytics and research support. Users are responsible for their own decisions.

### Do you handle customer funds or execute trades?

No. Market Signal AI does not custody funds, accept investment deposits, hold securities, connect to brokerage accounts for execution, execute trades or manage assets.

### How do customers pay?

Customers pay recurring SaaS subscription fees through Paddle as Merchant of Record. We plan to use Paddle checkout and Paddle Customer Portal for subscription management.

### How are refunds handled?

Refunds are reviewed under Market Signal AI's Refund Policy, Paddle's Merchant of Record process and applicable law. Duplicate charges, confirmed billing errors, unauthorized charges and material technical failures may be reviewed case by case.

### How can customers cancel?

Customers can cancel through the billing/account interface using Paddle Customer Portal. Cancellation stops future renewals. Paid access may continue until the end of the current paid billing period unless stated otherwise.

### What are the main payment risks?

The main risks are subscription disputes, failed payments, chargebacks and potential misunderstanding of market analytics as investment advice. Controls include clear recurring billing disclosure, self-service cancellation, refund policy, risk disclaimers, no personalized advice, no execution, no guaranteed returns and Paddle webhook lifecycle handling.
